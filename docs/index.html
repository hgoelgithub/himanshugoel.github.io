About me

Computational Scientist with 7+ years of experience at the intersection of predictive toxicology, drug design, and AI/ML for life sciences. I build end-to-end computational pipelines that turn complex biological and chemical data into actionable safety and efficacy insights.

In my most recent role at BHSAI / Henry M. Jackson Foundation (DoD, DTRA, NIH, AFRL funded), I led computational toxicology research across several core areas:

  • Data curation & visualization: Extracted actionable insights from large-scale chemical and biological datasets using PCA, t-SNE, UMAP, clustering, and heatmaps.
  • ML for compound classification: Trained DNN, GNN, and transformer LLM classifiers to predict compound activity at neurotoxicity-relevant GPCR targets.
  • Generative AI: Designed novel molecular structures and supported drug repurposing.
  • Neurotoxicity: Assessed 200+ chemicals using high-throughput Multi-Electrode Array (MEA) data, integrating dose-response modeling (tcplfit2) and chemical–protein interaction networks to build mechanistic toxicity profiles.
  • Toxicogenomics: Developed RNA-Seq pipelines across rat, guinea pig, and human hepatocyte systems — identifying biomarkers for liver and kidney injury and performing cross-species IVIVE analysis. Published in Int. J. Mol. Sci. (2023, 2024).
  • Integrative safety science: Connected genomics, bioinformatics, and toxicology to improve understanding of chemical impact on human health and strengthen safety assessments.
  • ADMET profiling: Conducted risk assessment of chemical compounds using vNN-ADMET and SwissADME.

Before that, I was a postdoctoral researcher at Dr. MacKerell’s CADD Center, University of Maryland, where I developed the SILCS drug discovery framework — including a novel water network modeling method (SILCS-MC Water) — and built predictive models for hERG cardiotoxicity and protein–ligand binding affinity.

I thrive in cross-functional environments, collaborating closely with biologists, toxicologists, and software engineers to translate in silico findings into real-world impact. I have a strong commitment to New Approach Methodologies (NAM) integrating in vitro assays, multi-OMICS, and computational models to reduce animal testing and improve translational safety prediction.

I’m currently open to new opportunities in computational toxicology, drug screening & discovery, or AI/ML for life sciences. Get in touch →
