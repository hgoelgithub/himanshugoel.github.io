Research Journey

Academic training, research highlights, and scientific contributions across institutions

Career & education timeline

  • 🔬
    Feb 2022 – Mar 2025
    Computational Research Scientist II
    BHSAI · Henry M. Jackson Foundation for the Advancement of Military Medicine · Frederick, MD
    • Data curation & visualization: Managed and visualized large-scale biological and chemical datasets — feature importance, data cleaning, PCA, t-SNE, UMAP, clustering, heatmaps, pattern recognition, and statistical interpretation.
    • Generative AI in drug discovery: Utilized generative AI techniques to generate novel molecular structures, enhancing model predictions and supporting drug repurposing applications.
    • ML models for compound classification: Developed traditional ML, DNN, GNN, and transformer-based LLM classifiers to identify active/inactive compounds for neurotoxicity-relevant GPCR targets using public databases.
    • Computational neurotoxicity assessment: Neurotoxicity assessment of 200+ chemicals using high-dimensional Multi-Electrode Array (MEA) assay data (electrical activity of neurons).
    • Integrative computational neurotoxicity profiling: Integrated high-throughput data with dose-response modeling (tcplfit2) to characterize neurotoxic effects; explored chemical structure–MEA response profile relationships; incorporated chemical–protein interactions and molecular features for integrative computational biology analyses.
    • Toxicogenomics studies: Developed and implemented RNA-Seq pipelines for in vivo and in vitro animal (rat, guinea pig) and human (pooled hepatocyte) data. Conducted DEG analysis for liver and kidney injury biomarkers. Performed in vitro–in vivo correlation and cross-species analysis to enhance translational relevance.
    • Integrating genomics, bioinformatics, and toxicology to enhance understanding of chemical impact on human health and improve safety assessments.
    • ADMET profiling with vNN-ADMET/SwissADME for risk assessment of chemical compounds.
    • Supported web development and program upgrades in collaboration with the IT team.
    • Collaborated with cross-functional teams — biologists, toxicologists, and software engineers — on DoD, DTRA, NIH, and AFRL funded projects.
  • ⚗️
    May 2018 – Feb 2022
    Postdoctoral Researcher
    Computer-Aided Drug Design Center · University of Maryland, Baltimore · Advisor: Prof. Alexander D. MacKerell Jr.
    • Computational drug discovery framework: Spearheaded development and deployment of computational workflows for early-phase drug discovery — protein structure preparation, SILCS FragMaps, hotspot analysis, pharmacophore modeling, virtual screening, molecular docking, and SAR analysis for hit identification and lead prioritization.
    • Protein-ligand binding affinity: Employed SILCS technology to compute protein-ligand relative binding affinities and benchmarked against current FEP methods to validate accuracy for drug design applications.
    • Polarizable force field and MD simulations: Designed SILCS workflow for polarizable force fields and MD to assess protein-ligand energetics, molecular interactions, and docking scores.
    • Water network modeling: Developed and validated SILCS-MC Water — a novel technique to accurately predict water networks in binding pockets and their impact on ligand binding affinity and pose.
    • Imaging probe development: Investigated alpha-synuclein fibril binding using polarizable SILCS calculations and SAR analysis to elucidate probe–target interactions.
    • COVID-19 drug discovery: Applied SILCS-based CADD workflow targeting SARS-CoV-2 spike protein for antiviral drug development as part of the drug discovery team.
    • Target-specific modeling: Designed predictive models for hERG1 blockade integrating SILCS modeling and physicochemical properties to support safety pharmacology profiling.
    • Partnered with multidisciplinary teams to facilitate data-driven decision making and translate in silico predictions to actionable insights.
  • 🎓
    Aug 2013 – May 2018
    PhD · Chemical Engineering
    Mississippi State University · Starkville, MS · Advisor: Prof. Neeraj Rai
    GPA 3.66 / 4.0

    Thesis: "Modeling Phase and Sorption Equilibria using First Principles Simulations"

    • VLE & thermodynamic properties: Predicted vapor–liquid equilibria (VLE), saturated vapor pressures, critical properties, boiling points, and structural properties via first-principles DFT/classical Monte Carlo simulations.
    • Adsorption & transport in ionic liquids: Predicted adsorption equilibria, transport properties, vibrational spectra, and structural mechanisms for ionic liquids with gases via first-principles simulations.
    • DFT exchange-correlation functionals: Employed multiple DFT functionals for computing condensed-phase properties and dimer energetics with high accuracy.
    • Algorithm development in CP2K: Developed novel "cluster translation moves" algorithm useful for sampling strongly associating fluids in the CP2K Monte Carlo suite.
    • Force field development: Classical TraPPE force field development for organic molecules.
    • Partition coefficient & free energy prediction: Predicted octanol-water partition coefficients and hydration/solvation free energies for NDMA and trichloroethylene — important environmental contaminants.
  • 🏛️
    Dec 2011 – Jul 2013
    M.Tech · Chemical Engineering
    Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad (IITH) · India
    CGPA 9.17 / 10.0
    • Interfacial science: Computed interfacial properties for multiple liquid systems via dissipative particle dynamics simulations.
    • Teaching Assistant: Advanced Chemical Engineering Thermodynamics, IITH (Aug 2012 – Dec 2012).
    • Laboratory Instructor: Introduction to Chemical Engineering Lab, IITH (Jan 2013 – May 2013).

Research highlights

Drug design & CADD (UMB · 2018–2022)

SILCS-based drug discovery framework

Spearheaded end-to-end computational workflows — protein structure preparation, SILCS FragMaps, hotspot analysis, pharmacophore modeling, virtual screening, molecular docking, and SAR analysis — for efficient hit identification and lead prioritization.

SILCSVirtual ScreeningDockingSAR

Protein–ligand binding affinity & FEP benchmarking

Employed SILCS to compute protein-ligand relative binding affinities and benchmarked against FEP methods to validate accuracy and enhance reliability for drug design. Published in Chem. Sci. 2021.

SILCSFEPBinding Affinity

SILCS-MC Water — novel water network modeling

Developed and validated a novel technique to accurately predict water networks in protein binding pockets and quantify their impact on ligand binding affinity and binding pose.

Water NetworksMonte CarloSILCS

hERG cardiotoxicity prediction

Designed predictive models for hERG1 blockade integrating SILCS modeling and physicochemical properties to support safety pharmacology profiling. Published in Chemistry 2022.

hERGSafety PharmacologyML

Polarizable force field & MD simulations

Designed SILCS workflow for Drude polarizable force fields and MD to assess protein-ligand energetics, molecular interactions, and docking scores with higher accuracy than additive force fields.

Polarizable FFMDCHARMM

COVID-19 antiviral & imaging probe development

Applied SILCS CADD workflow to SARS-CoV-2 spike protein for antiviral drug development. Separately investigated alpha-synuclein fibril binding using polarizable SILCS and SAR analysis for imaging probe development.

COVID-19Alpha-SynucleinCADD

Computational toxicology & AI/ML (BHSAI · 2022–2025)

Toxicogenomics & RNA-Seq pipelines

Developed RNA-Seq pipelines for in vivo (rat, guinea pig) and in vitro (human hepatocyte) data. Conducted DEG analysis for liver and kidney injury biomarkers. Performed in vitro–in vivo correlation and cross-species analysis to enhance translational relevance. Published in Int. J. Mol. Sci. 2023, 2024.

RNA-SeqKallistoIVIVECross-Species

MEA neurotoxicity & dose-response modeling

Neurotoxicity assessment of 200+ chemicals using high-dimensional Multi-Electrode Array (MEA) assay data. Integrated high-throughput data with dose-response modeling (tcplfit2) and explored chemical structure–MEA response profile similarity relationships.

MEAtcplfit2NeurotoxDose-Response

ML/DL models for GPCR compound classification

Developed traditional ML, DNN, GNN, and transformer-based LLM classifiers to identify active/inactive compounds against neurotoxicity-relevant GPCR targets. Leveraged compound libraries from public databases to enhance drug-target interaction predictions.

GNNTransformer LLMGPCRDeepChem

Generative AI for drug repurposing

Applied generative AI techniques to design novel molecular structures, enhancing model predictions and supporting drug repurposing applications with integrated ADMET profiling via vNN-ADMET/SwissADME.

Generative AIDrug RepurposingADMET

Data curation & chemical space visualization

Managed and visualized large-scale biological and chemical datasets using PCA, t-SNE, UMAP, clustering, and heatmaps. Performed feature importance, data cleaning, pattern recognition, and statistical interpretation across multi-OMICS datasets.

UMAPt-SNEMulti-OMICSCheminformatics

First-principles simulations (MSU PhD · 2013–2018)

Vapor–liquid equilibria via DFT & Monte Carlo

Predicted VLE, saturated vapor pressures, critical properties, and boiling points for hydrofluorocarbons, CO₂, SO₂, and argon using first-principles DFT/Monte Carlo in CP2K. Employed multiple DFT exchange-correlation functionals for condensed-phase properties. 7 publications.

DFTMonte CarloCP2KVLE

CO₂ absorption in ionic liquids

Predicted adsorption equilibria, transport properties, vibrational spectra, and structural mechanisms for CO₂ and other gases in phosphonium-based ionic liquids via first-principles simulations.

Ionic LiquidsCO₂ CaptureDFT

Algorithm development: cluster translation moves

Developed a novel "cluster translation moves" algorithm in the CP2K Monte Carlo suite, enabling efficient sampling of strongly associating fluids that were previously computationally intractable.

AlgorithmCP2KMonte Carlo

Force field development & environmental contaminants

Developed classical TraPPE force fields for chloroethenes and fluorinated alkanes. Predicted octanol-water partition coefficients and solvation free energies for NDMA and trichloroethylene — key environmental contaminants.

TraPPEForce FieldNDMAEnvironment

Interfacial science (IITH M.Tech · 2011–2013)

Interfacial properties via dissipative particle dynamics

Computed interfacial tension and related properties for multiple immiscible and partially miscible liquid systems using dissipative particle dynamics (DPD) simulations. Work published in Chem. Phys. Lett. 2014.

DPDInterfacial ScienceThermodynamics
