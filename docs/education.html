Career & education timeline

  • 🔬
    Feb 2022 – Mar 2025

    BHSAI · Henry M. Jackson Foundation for the Advancement of Military Medicine · Frederick, MD
    • Data curation & visualization: Managed and visualized large-scale biological and chemical datasets — feature importance, data cleaning, PCA, t-SNE, UMAP, clustering, heatmaps, pattern recognition, and statistical interpretation.

Conducted DEG analysis for liver and kidney injury biomarkers. Performed in vitro–in vivo correlation and cross-species analysis to enhance translational relevance.
    • Integrating genomics, bioinformatics, and toxicology to enhance understanding of chemical impact on human health and improve safety assessments.
    • ADMET profiling with vNN-ADMET/SwissADME for risk assessment of chemical compounds.
    • Supported web development and program upgrades in collaboration with the IT team.
    • Collaborated with cross-functional teams — biologists, toxicologists, and software engineers — on DoD, DTRA, NIH, and AFRL funded projects.
  • ⚗️
    May 2018 – Feb 2022
    Postdoctoral Researcher
    Computer-Aided Drug Design Center · University of Maryland, Baltimore · Advisor: Prof. Alexander D. MacKerell Jr.
    • Computational drug discovery framework: Spearheaded development and deployment of computational workflows for early-phase drug discovery — protein structure preparation, SILCS FragMaps, hotspot analysis, pharmacophore modeling, virtual screening, molecular docking, and SAR analysis for hit identification and lead prioritization.

    • Imaging probe development: Investigated alpha-synuclein fibril binding using polarizable SILCS calculations and SAR analysis to elucidate probe–target interactions.
    • COVID-19 drug discovery: Applied SILCS-based CADD workflow targeting SARS-CoV-2 spike protein for antiviral drug development as part of the drug discovery team.
    • Target-specific modeling: Designed predictive models for hERG1 blockade integrating SILCS modeling and physicochemical properties to support safety pharmacology profiling.
    • Partnered with multidisciplinary teams to facilitate data-driven decision making and translate in silico predictions to actionable insights.
  • 🎓

    PhD · Chemical Engineering
    Mississippi State University · Starkville, MS · Advisor: Prof. Neeraj Rai
    GPA 3.66 / 4.0

    Thesis: "Modeling Phase and Sorption Equilibria using First Principles Simulations"

    • VLE & thermodynamic properties: Predicted vapor–liquid equilibria (VLE), saturated vapor pressures, critical properties, boiling points, and structural properties via first-principles DFT/classical Monte Carlo simulations.
    • Adsorption & transport in ionic liquids: Predicted adsorption equilibria, transport properties, vibrational spectra, and structural mechanisms for ionic liquids with gases via first-principles simulations.
    • DFT exchange-correlation functionals: Employed multiple DFT functionals for computing condensed-phase properties and dimer energetics with high accuracy.
    • Algorithm development in CP2K: Developed novel "cluster translation moves" algorithm useful for sampling strongly associating fluids in the CP2K Monte Carlo suite.
    • Force field development: Classical TraPPE force field development for organic molecules.
    • Partition coefficient & free energy prediction: Predicted octanol-water partition coefficients and hydration/solvation free energies for NDMA and trichloroethylene — important environmental contaminants.
