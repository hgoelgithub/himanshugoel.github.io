Himanshu Goel — Computational Scientist

📄 Download PDF version   💼 LinkedIn   📧 hgchemical@gmail.com

Professional summary

Experienced Computational Scientist with over 7 years of interdisciplinary experience in computational chemistry, biology, and toxicology. Strong background in computer-aided drug design, predictive toxicology, cheminformatics, bioinformatics, toxicogenomics, molecular dynamics, Monte Carlo, DFT, RNA-Seq, high-throughput screening, multiOMICS, and real-world datasets. Proficient in deep learning, transfer learning, generative AI, LLM, RAG, and agentic AI applied to real-world applications.

Work experience

Computational Research Scientist II

BHSAI, Henry M. Jackson Foundation for the Advancement of Military Medicine · Frederick, MD
Feb 2022 – Mar 2025

  • Data curation & visualization of large-scale biological and chemical datasets (PCA, t-SNE, UMAP, clustering, heatmaps)
  • Generative AI for novel molecular structure generation and drug repurposing
  • ML models (DNN, GNN, transformer LLMs) for compound classification against neurotoxicity-relevant GPCR targets
  • Computational neurotoxicity assessment of 200+ chemicals via Multi-Electrode Array (MEA) assay data
  • Integrative toxicogenomics: RNA-Seq pipelines for in vivo/in vitro rat, guinea pig, and human hepatocyte data
  • DEG analysis for liver/kidney injury biomarkers; in vitro–in vivo correlation and cross-species analysis
  • ADMET profiling with vNN-ADMET/SwissADME for risk assessment
  • Funded by DoD, DTRA, NIH, and AFRL

Postdoctoral Researcher

Computer-Aided Drug Design Center, University of Maryland Baltimore · Baltimore, MD
May 2018 – Feb 2022
Advisor: Prof. Alexander D. MacKerell, Jr.

  • Developed and deployed computational workflows for early-phase drug discovery: protein prep, SILCS FragMaps, pharmacophore modeling, virtual screening, docking, SAR analysis
  • Benchmarked SILCS protein-ligand relative binding affinities against FEP methods
  • Designed SILCS workflow for polarizable force fields and MD simulations
  • Developed SILCS-MC Water — a novel technique to predict water networks in binding pockets
  • COVID-19: Applied SILCS CADD workflow targeting SARS-CoV-2 spike protein for antiviral drug development
  • hERG1 safety pharmacology: predictive models integrating SILCS + physicochemical properties

Research Assistant

Mississippi State University · Starkville, MS
Aug 2013 – May 2018
Advisor: Prof. Neeraj Rai

  • Predicted vapor-liquid equilibria (VLE), saturated vapor pressures, and critical properties via DFT/Monte Carlo
  • Adsorption equilibria, transport properties, and structural mechanisms for ionic liquids
  • Novel “cluster translation moves” algorithm in CP2K MC suite
  • TraPPE classical force field development for organic molecules
  • Octanol-water partition coefficients and solvation free energies for NDMA and trichloroethylene

Research Assistant

Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad · Hyderabad, India

  • Interfacial properties for multiple liquid systems via dissipative particle dynamics
